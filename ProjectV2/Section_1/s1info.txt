Group means
Male mean BPXSY ~124.7, Female ~120.3 — males higher on average.
Young 15–30 mean ~111.7, Old 30–80 mean ~124.3 — a roughly 12-mmHg gap that is very large relative to a ~17.9 std.

Age is a dominant driver — any hypothesis test or regression must condition on or include age.
Gender difference in BPXSY is visible in the descriptive stats and merits a formal test (Section 3 Test 1).
Young vs old separation is enormous — Section 3 Test 2 is almost certainly going to reject H0 (confirmed below).
Because distributions are right-skewed, CIs and t-tests rely on CLT/large-n rather than normality; n is large enough for that to be safe (Section 2, 3).
